Reverend Clinton J. Hall II, Pastor

Reverend Clinton J. Hall II, Pastor



Reverend Clinton J. Hall II is the son of the late Clinton J. Hall, Senior and Minne Mae Wright Hall of Greensboro, Georgia. He was educated in the public school system of Green County Georgia. He did undergraduate studies at FT. Valley State College, S.R. Higgins Seminary, Ambassador Seminary and S.C. Criminal Justice Academy.

He is the founder of Destiny World Ministries, a former Vice President for the United Food and Commerical Workers International Union, and he was a former educator in the school system of Abbeville County, South Carolina.

In 1997 David Beasley, Governor of South Carolina, did appoint and the Full Senate did confirm him as the first African American Judge of Abbeville County, South Carolina that dealt with the day to day judicial work for the state.

In 2005, the town of Calhoun Falls appointed him as the first African American judge in its history.

Reverend Clinton J. Hall II is the pastor of Bethany African Methodist Episcopal Church and he is the father of three children, Clinton III, Joshua, and Ikia.
